What Is Calculus?

Calculus is a branch of mathematics focused on change and motion. It is divided into two main sections:

  • Differential Calculus – Concerned with the concept of a derivative, which represents the rate of change.
  • Integral Calculus – Focused on the accumulation of quantities, typically represented as areas under curves.

Understanding these concepts is critical, as calculus forms the foundation for fields like physics, engineering, economics, and data science.
